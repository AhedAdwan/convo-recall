## Prohibited Uses

The Software may NOT be used in or for any of the following:

### 1. Government use

Use by, on behalf of, or for the direct benefit of any government institution
at any level — national, federal, state, provincial, regional, county, or
municipal government; any public agency; or any quasi-public body.

**Carve-out:** public schools, public colleges, and public universities are
permitted to use the Software *solely* for teaching and academic research.
Government-funded grants flowing to private universities or non-government
research institutions remain permitted; the prohibition is on operational
government use, not on academic research funding sources.

### 2. Military, defense, weapons, intelligence, armed-conflict use

Use in or for any military service, defense system, weapons system,
intelligence service, or armed-conflict purpose. This includes — but is not
limited to — reconnaissance; targeting; autonomous-weapons control;
command-and-control systems; lethal autonomous decision systems; and any
training, deployment, or evaluation thereof.

### 3. Mass-surveillance, social-credit, and biometric-identification systems

Use in any system whose primary purpose is identifying individuals from a
general population without their specific informed consent. This includes
mass-surveillance systems, social-credit scoring systems, and biometric
identification systems operated against a general population.

**Not prohibited:** consent-based identity systems used for the user's own
authentication (e.g., FaceID on a personal device), retail loyalty programs
where individuals opt in, or research conducted with informed consent under an
ethics review board.

## Why these rules

convo-recall indexes private conversation history. The author considers the
ability to preserve memory across AI agent sessions a personal-empowerment
tool, not a surveillance instrument. These prohibitions exist to ensure the
Software is not weaponized against the same individuals who would benefit
from it.
