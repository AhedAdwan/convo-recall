| TD-001 | Active sidecar watcher: `embed_service` periodically scans the DB for un-embedded rows and embeds them in the background, eliminating the chain race entirely. Today the sidecar is purely reactive — it only embeds when something POSTs to `/embed`. The chain wait-for-socket fix (commit `<TBD>`) closes the race for the wizard's hot path, but a fully self-driving sidecar would make the entire system robust against any timing or watcher failure (e.g. ingest happening while sidecar was briefly down for maintenance). | TD-003 | systemd `.path` units (`PathChanged=` / `PathModified=`) are non-recursive by design — confirmed by `man systemd.path` ("with `PathModified=/var/www/html`, a change in `/var/www/html/dirxx/file.txt` will not trigger the script") and Lennart Poettering's stance ([systemd #4246](https://github.com/systemd/systemd/issues/4246)): "inotify watches are a limited resource [and] this doesn't really scale, and we hence won't do it." Effect: file appends inside `~/.claude/projects/<flat-cwd>/<sid>.jsonl` don't fire the parent-dir watcher we install. macOS launchd's `WatchPaths` IS recursive, so this is Linux-only. **Worked around in Phase 1** by adding response-completion ingest hooks on each CLI (Claude `Stop`, Gemini `AfterAgent`, Codex `Stop`). Phase 2 future: demote systemd `.path` to opt-in. Phase 3 future: replace with `Type=simple recall watch` supervisor. | 2026-05-02 | Medium | structural | auto_confirmed | — | Open |
| TD-004 | (MITIGATED) `apsw.BusyError: database is locked` raised in `_enable_wal_mode` (`ingest.py:249`) during first `recall install`. **Root cause CONFIRMED experimentally 2026-05-02**: race between two detached subprocesses spawned by the wizard, both calling `_enable_wal_mode`: (a) `recall _backfill-chain` started as `Popen(start_new_session=True)` at `_wizard.py:373`, (b) `recall watch` started per-agent at `_wizard.py:403`.
