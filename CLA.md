attachment, or any similar channel — you are agreeing to the terms below for
that contribution. If you cannot or do not agree to these terms, please do
not submit the contribution.

You can signal acceptance in either of two ways:

1. **Sign-off in the commit message** (Developer Certificate of Origin

   in every commit you contribute, asserting that you wrote the code (or
   have the right to submit it) under these terms. Many projects use
   `git commit -s` for this.

2. **Explicit acceptance in the pull request** by writing "I agree to the
   convo-recall CLA" (or equivalent) in the PR body or a comment.

Either form has the same effect.

## Why this exists

convo-recall is licensed under the [Functional Source License v1.1
(Apache-2.0 future)](LICENSE), with an [Acceptable Use
Policy](ACCEPTABLE_USE.md). The maintainer offers, and intends to continue
offering, separate commercial licenses to companies whose use cases the FSL
does not permit (e.g., bundling convo-recall in a commercial product).

Without this CLA, each contributor's copyright in their contribution would
limit the maintainer's ability to grant such commercial licenses (because the
contribution would be available *only* under the FSL, not under any other
terms the maintainer chooses to offer). This CLA is the standard mechanism
projects use to keep dual-licensing viable as the contributor base grows.
